## 1. How you used Generative AI
The team used generative AI to help update the formatting of the project. The team wanted all of the different HTML pages to look professional. The team figured that the best way to do this was to have generative AI look at some example images of what we wanted, and then go through our HTML pages and remake them to look good.
The second section, where we used generative AI, was in our JavaScript sections. This made the team's life a lot easier as we found coding in JavaScript to be our team's weakness. The AI (ChatGPT) helped the team write the two sections.

## 2. Impact on your development process
The AI tools that we used helped us increase our productivity by doing tasks that would have taken a long time in a couple of minutes. This also allowed us to collaborate a lot more on the harder sections where we did not use AI. The AI did not change our responsibilities because we only used it for sections that we left for the AI. All of the other work was still divided equally. Overall, the AI tools that we used saved a lot of time with tasks like making the whole final project look better.

## 3. Evaluation of benefits and drawbacks
The most useful aspects of generative AI are the time that it saves. It allows the team to do tasks that would take hours in minutes. I feel as if there are very few limitations when using AI. It is not always the greatest at making the best structured and simple code; however, it still gets the tasks done. Rechecking the AI content is important, for sure. There are many times where it will make up variable names, or give classes attributes that they do not have.

## 4. Your overall perspective
The use of AI tools requires regulation. If you are a person who relies solely on AI to solve all your problems, then you are hindering your own learning. It is very important not to abuse AI use when it is allowed in a class. I would definitely recommend students to limit their own AI use on projects, homework, and other assignments. Learning is very important; plugging in homework to ChatGPT does not enable any learning. AI should be limited to select cases where you want to get a task done in a short amount of time, or want to use the AI to benefit your learning, like explaining a topic or assignment.
